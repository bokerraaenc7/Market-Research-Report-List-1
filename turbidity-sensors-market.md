<p><p>Turbidity sensors are essential for measuring water clarity, with two primary types dominating the market: analog and digital turbidity sensors. Analog turbidity sensors provide continuous voltage or current output proportional to the turbidity level, making them simpler and cost-effective for basic applications. On the other hand, digital turbidity sensors use advanced processing to deliver precise readings, often incorporating features like data logging and connectivity options. This differentiates them in terms of accuracy and functionality, catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>
